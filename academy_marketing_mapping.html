Why June 30 for Mapping?

Industry standard for a new marketing function to complete a full audit-to-framework build is 8–12 weeks. We're inside that window. June 30 gives enough lead time to begin Building before the critical Summer 2026 campaign season ends — so Building assets are ready for AY26-27 mid-year push and Summer 2027 planning.

Why October for Scaling?

Scaling isn't possible without at least one full AY cycle of data to learn from. Declaring Scaling in October 2026 is honest: by then we'll have real performance signals from the first campaigns built in Q3. That's when we can confidently say the machine is running, not just built.

What's happening in parallel?

Mapping and early Building overlap by design. GTM playbooks for new campuses (Austin, Cambridge, Sugar Land) are already underway. This parallel-track approach is intentional — the framework informs new work, but existing urgent work doesn't pause for framework completion.

What does Mapping actually deliver?

Three outputs: (1) A finalized campus cohort model with assignments. (2) A playbook architecture doc defining what lives where. (3) A channel strategy matrix by cohort. These become the brief that guides everything built in Q3.

  • Northbrook open house: 125 people. Pasadena In-N-Out: 75 assessments

👥 CD Bandwidth Reality

  • CDs are overwhelmed — marketing bandwidth at campus level is near zero
  • Sean (Sugar Land) is brand new and alone. Scot (Pasadena) started in days
  • Any ask of CDs must be extremely low-lift to get done
  • Campbell: parent referral, social, email optimization, and partnerships are the top things marketing should own — not campuses

🏫 Three Distinct Campus Stages

  • New/growing (Sugar Land, Pasadena, Austin): scrappy startup mode, need everything
  • Established but struggling (Bellevue, Princeton, Santa Clara): awareness exists, funnel leaking
  • Strong/capacity-constrained (Northbrook, Frisco, Lexington): need retention strategy, not volume
  • Veteran retention-influx trend has broken for second consecutive year

  • RSM is the universal competitor — present everywhere, and families are leaving RSM for AoPS
  • Language arts is an underused differentiator: families switch from RSM specifically for LA

  • Demographics shape messaging; Korean vs. Indian vs. Chinese-American families respond differently

📧 Email & Data: Fragmented

  • No one knows how many emails go out per campus — no tracking
  • Salesforce is not connected to ActiveCampaign
  • Sam (RGP): wants a standardized campus marketing checklist, not strategy
  • Jalaja: consultation requests outperform mailing list capture — 200 requests for Austin pre-open
  • Minimum 2x/month email cadence is the established floor, rarely met

Campus Cohort Model

Three Cohorts.
One Living Model.

Campuses are grouped by enrollment health stage — not by year opened, not by region. Region is an overlay for message localization, not the primary organizing principle. Cohort assignment is not fixed: campuses advance as their health improves.

Why health stage, not age?

Northbrook (older, strong) and Sugar Land (new, building) share almost nothing tactically. But Sugar Land and Johns Creek — both new, high-awareness-need, CD-constrained — share everything. Grouping by stage means the playbook you hand a campus is immediately relevant to where they actually are, not a generic template they have to adapt.

💡

The scalability principle: Campuses handle localized tasks (confirming event date, posting from a template, forwarding a flyer). Marketing owns everything else: channel activation, asset creation, email deployment, paid media, playbook design. This is how a 25-campus network gets consistent marketing without 25 separate marketing managers. The playbook is the franchise manual. The CD is the local operator.
